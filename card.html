The Platypus

The platypus is the sole living representative of its family and genus alive today.

  • Scientific Name: Ornithorhynchus anatinus
  • Average Length: 18 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 17 years
  • Location: Eastern Australia and Tasmania

Platypus, also called duckbill, a small amphibious Australian mammal noted for its odd combination of primitive features and special adaptations, especially the flat, almost comical bill that early observers thought was that of a duck sewn onto the body of a mammal. The platypus is common in waterways of eastern Australia, where it generally feeds on bottom-dwelling invertebrates but also takes an occasional frog, fish, or insect at the water’s surface. Despite being a mammal, the platypus lays eggs. Source: Encyclopædia Britannica
